1. Introduction to the Conflict

The India-Pakistan conflict began shortly after both countries gained independence in 1947. The primary cause was the disputed territory of Kashmir, leading to several wars and ongoing tensions.

2. Key Wars in the Conflict

The 1965 War was a major military conflict between India and Pakistan, particularly over Kashmir.

The 1971 War led to the creation of Bangladesh and was a pivotal moment in the region's history.

The 1947-48 conflict saw the first military engagement between India and Pakistan over Kashmir.

3. Terrorist Attacks & Proxy Wars

  • ⚫ 2008 Mumbai Attacks (26/11)
  • ⚫ 2016 Pathankot & Uri attacks

4. India’s Military Response

India has responded to the conflict with military operations such as Surgical Strikes (2016) and Balakot Air Strike (2019) to target terror camps in Pakistan.

5. The Kashmir Dispute

The region of Kashmir has remained the focal point of the conflict. The UN has called for a plebiscite to resolve the issue, but it remains unresolved to this day.

6. Impact on Civilians

The ongoing conflict and cross-border terrorism have caused immense suffering to civilians on both sides. Thousands have lost their lives in the violence.

7. Hope for Peace

While the conflict remains unresolved, many hope for peace through international diplomacy, continued peace talks, and better relations between the two nations.
