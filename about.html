Understanding the India-Pakistan Conflict

A comprehensive timeline of key events, wars, and diplomatic efforts between the two nations since 1947

1Origins of the Conflict

The India-Pakistan conflict traces its roots to the partition of British India in 1947, which created two independent dominions: India and Pakistan. The princely state of Jammu and Kashmir, with its Muslim-majority population but Hindu ruler, became the primary flashpoint.

Key factors in the ongoing conflict include:

  • The disputed accession of Jammu & Kashmir to India
  • Three major wars (1947-48, 1965, 1971) and the Kargil conflict (1999)
  • Cross-border terrorism and proxy warfare
  • Nuclearization of both countries (1998)

2Major Wars Between India and Pakistan

1947-48 War

The first war over Kashmir began when tribal militias supported by Pakistan invaded the princely state. This resulted in the division of Kashmir along the Line of Control (LoC).

1965 War

A 17-day war initiated by Pakistan over Kashmir, ending in stalemate and the Tashkent Agreement.

1971 War

Resulted in Pakistan's surrender and creation of Bangladesh. Over 90,000 Pakistani troops were taken prisoner.

1999 Kargil Conflict

Pakistan's infiltration into Indian-administered Kashmir led to a limited war that India won, solidifying the LoC.

3Terrorism & Proxy Conflict

Since the 1980s, Pakistan has supported militant groups operating in Indian-administered Kashmir, leading to:

2001 Parliament Attack

Lashkar-e-Taiba and Jaish-e-Mohammed terrorists attacked the Indian Parliament, nearly bringing the two nuclear powers to war.

2008 Mumbai Attacks

10 Lashkar-e-Taiba terrorists conducted coordinated attacks across Mumbai, killing 166 people over four days.

2016 Uri Attack

Jaish-e-Mohammed militants attacked an Indian Army base in Uri, killing 19 soldiers and prompting Indian surgical strikes.

2019 Pulwama Attack

A suicide bomber killed 40 CRPF personnel, leading to the Balakot airstrike and aerial dogfight.

4India's Military Responses

India has developed a doctrine of proactive response to cross-border terrorism:

2016 Surgical Strikes

Following the Uri attack, Indian commandos conducted cross-LoC strikes destroying multiple terror launch pads.

2019 Balakot Airstrike

IAF jets struck a Jaish-e-Mohammed training camp in Balakot, Pakistan in response to Pulwama attack.

Counter-Insurgency Ops

Ongoing military operations in Jammu & Kashmir against Pakistan-supported militants.

5The Kashmir Dispute

The Himalayan region remains the core issue between India and Pakistan:

Territorial Claims

Both countries claim the entire former princely state, controlling parts separated by the Line of Control.

UN Resolution

The 1948 UN resolution called for a plebiscite after Pakistan withdrew its forces, which never happened.

Current Status

India revoked Jammu & Kashmir's special status in 2019, further escalating tensions with Pakistan.

Pathways to Peace

Despite ongoing tensions, several peace initiatives have been attempted:

  • Simla Agreement (1972): Established the Line of Control and bilateral dispute resolution
  • Lahore Declaration (1999): Vowed to resolve all issues including Kashmir
  • Composite Dialogue (2004-08): Addressed all outstanding issues including terrorism
  • Backchannel Diplomacy: Ongoing secret talks between intelligence agencies
